Platform credits are a **consumption metering unit for paid compute and services** rather than an SI‑traceable entitlement to fixed physical CPU cores forever. Practical meaning: each simulation, notebook execution, or billed task draws down credits according to internally defined job weighting (solver time, parallelism, allocated resources).

There is typically **no simple universal identity** such as “1 credit ≡ X vCPU‑hours regardless of workload”—throughput differs with simulator settings, partitioning, queues, discounts, organisation pools, etc. Consultants should cite the authoritative **Credits & pricing documentation** surfaced via the assistant/tools, summarise what that page guarantees today, steer users toward in‑platform cost hints or quotas where available, and avoid inventing undocumented conversion factors.
